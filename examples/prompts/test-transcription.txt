Deep in the heart of the Plush Forest, where the mushrooms grow taller than houses and the flowers hum soft lullabies, there lived a curious explorer with the softest pair of rabbit ears you ever saw.

Her name was Mira. And today — today she was going somewhere no one had gone before.

The path curled ahead like a ribbon left by a dreaming giant, winding between roots as wide as rivers and petals that shimmered in every color the sky forgot to keep. Every step she took left a little glow in the moss, as if the forest was glad she came.

A lantern seed drifted past her nose. She giggled and spun around to watch it float up, up, up — until it vanished into the canopy like a secret whispered into the trees.

There were sounds here you could only hear if you were very still. The slow tick of a dew drop. The breath of the bioluminescent blooms as they opened one petal at a time. The distant rustling of something small and friendly, watching from behind a velvet leaf.

Mira's ears swiveled. Her tail gave a hopeful flick.

She was not afraid. The Plush Forest had never given her a reason to be.

She pressed forward, deeper into the golden afternoon light — past the spiral gate of twisted bark, past the river that ran uphill on Tuesdays, past the great sleeping Fungus of Seven Faces who wore a different expression depending on the weather.

And when she finally reached the clearing — the one she had only ever seen in dreams — she stopped.

It was exactly how she imagined it. And nothing like it at all.

The flowers here were enormous, soft, and impossibly bright. Firefly lanterns hung in clusters between the branches. The air tasted like warm honey and something she couldn't name but had been looking for her entire life.

Mira sat down on a mossy log, right in the middle of all of it.

She breathed in. She breathed out. Her ears went still.

And for one long, shining, perfect moment — the whole world was plush and warm and hers.
